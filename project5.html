Day Zero

The day a city’s taps run dry, the final stage of unsolved water scarcity.

By 2025, of the world’s population will be living in water stressed areas

Water scarcity is a growing risk worldwide, damaging health, education and food resources everwhere it affects.



Solution

A high-fidelity audiovisual exhibit using interactive data to educate visitors on the importance and urgency of water scarcity.



Primary attraction #1:

An interactive globe where visitors can view water data on a worldwide scale



Visualized water usage






Visitors will see water usage separated into agriculture, industry, and home use.



Expandable info points on critical water areas



Visitors will be able to expand on certain points of the map, reading into more detail on certain countries that are particularly important.



Primary attraction #2:

An interactive timeline allowing visitors to see the progression of water availability throughout time.






Visitors will be able to see water scarcity levels from 1962 to current day, as well as projections for 2020,2030, and 2040



Primary attraction #3:

A protoype water desalinator that can run off a singular solar panel.



Visitors will be able to view cost estimates and general facts about the distillation and membrane processes. They will also be able to see the distillation process in action.






Takeaways

Interactive water survey






We will use water as an interactive element, surveying how visitors use water by letting them pour water into different containers.



WHY ARE WE DOING THIS?


Humans need water to live.

Day Zero is coming.

It’s sooner than you think, and closer to home than you realize.
